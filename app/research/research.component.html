Research

My research mainly involves image segmentation and anlysis on nervous tissue, for both light-microscopy and FIB-SEM level.
Instead of directly employing the recent deep learning algorithms, we go back to the traditional methods that take intensity and shape into consideration more intuitively.

  • Motivation
  • Mitochondria
  • Glycogen
  • Blood Vessels

Why not using deep learning algorithms directly?


High-quality training data is particularly hard to acquire in medical domain.
For segmentation problem particulary, manaully labelling for the ground-truth is extremely time-consuming.
Also, for a 3D volume, a trained expert needs to go back and forth in the nearby slices, checking if it is indeed the desired structure.


Instead, we aim to generate training data using traditional computer vision methods. Then feed the output into neural networks.

We use the active contour methods with level-set formulation.
